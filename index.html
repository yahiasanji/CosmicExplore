Frequently Asked Questions

What is a black hole?

Answer: A black hole is a region of space where gravity is so intense that nothing can escape from it, not even light. It forms when a massive star collapses in on itself at the end of its life. Beyond the event horizon (the boundary of the black hole), everything that enters is trapped forever.

What is dark matter?

Answer: Dark matter is an invisible form of matter that cannot be detected directly because it doesn't emit, absorb, or reflect light. It makes up about 27% of the universe and can only be detected through its gravitational effects on visible matter. Its exact nature remains one of science's greatest mysteries.

Is there life elsewhere in the universe?

Answer: We don't know for certain yet, but the odds are high. Over 5,000 exoplanets have been discovered, some of which are in the "habitable zone" of their star where liquid water could exist. Missions to Europa (Jupiter's moon) and Enceladus (Saturn's moon) are searching for signs of microbial life within our own solar system.

How long would it take to travel to Mars?

Answer: With current technology, a trip to Mars takes about 6 to 9 months, depending on the relative positions of the two planets. The distance between Earth and Mars varies between 55 million and 400 million kilometers. Future propulsion systems could reduce this time to just a few weeks.

Can we travel faster than light?

Answer: According to Einstein's theory of relativity, nothing can travel faster than light in a vacuum (approximately 300,000 km/s). However, theoretical concepts like the "warp drive" could potentially bypass this limit by bending spacetime itself, though this remains science fiction for now.
